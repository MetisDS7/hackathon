# Hypothesis Generation
Here are a list of resources that may help shake you out of writer's block, when trying to come up with a compelling idea for a data science research project.

* [Creative Hypothesis Generating in Psychology - Some Useful Heuristics](http://www.annualreviews.org/doi/pdf/10.1146/annurev.psych.48.1.1)
* [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com)
* [Coursolve - Data Science Projects](https://www.coursolve.org/browse-needs?query=Data%20Science)
* [DataKind Projects](http://www.datakind.org/projects)
* [MyNASAData](http://mynasadata.larc.nasa.gov/#)
* [DATA.GOV - Applications](http://www.data.gov/applications)
* [Echoprint - Data](http://echoprint.me/data)
* [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com)
* [Big Data: Too Many Answers, Not Enough Questions](http://www.datasciencecentral.com/profiles/blogs/big-data-too-many-answers-not-enough-questions)
* [That's Interesting: Towards a Phenomenology of Sociology and a Sociology of Phenomenology](http://www-personal.umich.edu/~samoore/teach/bit8852005/files/72362.pdf)
* [William Shockley on what makes a person who publishes a lot of papers (and the superstar researcher system)](https://dynamicecology.wordpress.com/2014/01/23/william-shockley-on-what-makes-a-person-write-a-lot-of-papers-and-the-superstar-researcher-system/)
